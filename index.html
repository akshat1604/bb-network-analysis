Techniques and Terminologies:

When working on network analysis, I've employed a range of techniques and concepts that may seem daunting at first glance. However, by breaking down these terms into simpler explanations, even those unfamiliar with computer science can grasp their significance:
Named Entity Recognition (NER):
Named Entity Recognition is a technique used to identify and classify entities within text data. In the context of Breaking Bad, NER helps us extract specific information such as character names, locations, and organizations mentioned in episode summaries or character descriptions.
Centrality Measures:
Network Centrality: Network centrality refers to the importance of a node (or character in our case) within a network. It quantifies how central or influential a character is in the overall structure of the Breaking Bad universe.
Degree Centrality: Degree centrality measures how well-connected a character is by counting the number of connections (or edges) they have with other characters. A character with high degree centrality is highly connected and may play a significant role in the narrative.
Betweenness Centrality: Betweenness centrality identifies characters that act as bridges or intermediaries between other characters in the network. These characters often control the flow of information or influence within the Breaking Bad universe.
Closeness Centrality: Closeness centrality measures how quickly a character can reach all other characters in the network. Characters with high closeness centrality are central to the network's communication and interaction dynamics.
Eigen Centrality: Eigen centrality evaluates a character's importance based on their connections to other important characters. It takes into account not only a character's direct connections but also the connections of their connections.
PageRank: PageRank, originally developed by Google, assesses the importance of a character based on the quality and quantity of their connections. Characters with high PageRank are considered influential within the network.
These terminologies served as essential tools in my analysis, enabling me to quantify and understand the roles and relationships of characters within the Breaking Bad universe. Through their application, I gained valuable insights into the dynamics that drive the narrative and shape the interconnected web of characters in the series.
